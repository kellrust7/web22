Topic 1: Mead - A History & Guide

I have a couple of friends who make and sell homemade mead, and are even having their products packaged to be sold in stores. When I tried mead for the first time (their homemade mead), it was sweet and smooth - very enjoyable! Everyone knows about whiskies, rums, vodkas, beers, wines, etc., but what about mead? Like many, I had never even heard of it until just recently. So, the goal of this webpage is to give mead a moment in the spotlight. Here are my hero and section ideas:

  • Hero: Illustration/image & quick blurb (above, in progress)
  • Section 1: What is mead? What is it made of and how is it made (process): a step-by-step
  • Section 2: A brief history of mead: a story
  • Section 3: Types of meads: a glossary & guide
  • Section 4: How/where is mead most relevant today?
  • Section 5: Well-known meaderies/establishments: a list

Topic 2: The History of School Lunches

Meals in general were quite different in the past, especially in terms of accessibility to food products, as well as the types of jobs people had. Since in the 19th and 20th centuries more families were living in rural farmland areas, meals were able to be had at home during the workday. Lunch, used interchangeably with “supper” back then, was actually the most significant meal of the day, compared to dinner now.

  • Hero: School lunch image / lunchbox illustration
  • Section 1: - A brief history of meals in the U.S. during the 19th century
  • Section 2: - How school lunches came to be; Committees/Organizations for School Lunch & Child Welfare
  • Section 3: - Challenges & methods for serving school lunches in the 19th century; innovations
  • Section 4: - The affect of school lunches on the youth social sphere & culture at school
  • Section 5: - School lunch advocacy today: the (now obsolete) food pyramid & Michelle Obama’s food plans, got milk?, organizations for providing lunches to impoverished/homeless students
